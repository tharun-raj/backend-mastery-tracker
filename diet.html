How to Hit 10,000 Steps
10,000 steps = ~7–8 km = ~80–100 minutes of total walking per day. You don't need to do it all at once — here's how to split it across your office day.

Your Daily Step Schedule (Fits Around Office 9–6)
6:00 AM
Morning walk
🌅 Morning Walk — 20 min (~2,500 steps)
Walk to gym or around your area. Brisk pace. This also warms up your body before training. On rest days: still do this — it's active recovery, not extra stress.
9–6 PM
Office hours
🏢 Office Movement — ~3,500–4,000 steps
Park further away or get off bus 1 stop early (+500 steps). Use stairs instead of lift (+300/day). Walk to colleagues instead of calling/messaging. Take calls standing and pacing. These passive steps add up to ~3,500–4,000 without any dedicated walk time.
1:00 PM
Lunch break
🍽️ Post-Lunch Walk — 15 min (~1,800 steps)
Walk after eating lunch — this is scientifically proven to reduce post-meal blood sugar spikes by up to 30%. Improves insulin sensitivity which directly helps fat loss. Even 10 min around the office block counts.
6:30 PM
Evening
🌆 Evening Walk / Commute — 20 min (~2,500 steps)
Walk home from office or after evening gym. If you gymmed in the evening, your gym session itself counts for ~2,000–3,000 steps. So this can be a 10 min walk instead. On office days: walk during commute back home.
After dinner
Optional
🌙 Post-Dinner Walk — 10 min (~1,200 steps)
Light 10 min walk after dinner = aids digestion, improves overnight fat burning, and reduces post-dinner blood sugar. Don't skip this — it's the easiest 1,200 steps of the day. Just outside your building is enough.
📊 Daily Step Total
🌅 Morning: ~2,500 🏢 Office passive: ~3,500–4,000 🍽️ Lunch walk: ~1,800 🌆 Evening: ~2,500 🌙 After dinner: ~1,200
Total: ~11,500 steps/day — no dedicated "exercise walk" needed. All fits around your office schedule.
Start Smart
If you're currently at ~3,000–4,000 steps, don't jump to 10,000 on day 1. Add 1,500 steps per week. Week 1: 5,000. Week 2: 6,500. Week 3: 8,000. Week 4: 10,000. This prevents knee and shin strain.
Track It
Use Google Fit (free), Apple Health, or a ₹500 Mi Band. Tracking increases your steps by ~2,500/day automatically — you unconsciously walk more when you can see the number. Cheap investment, huge result.
Pumpkin Seeds — Best Time to Eat
Yes, evening/dinner is actually the best time. Here's the science behind it and how to use them in your plan.
✅ Your Instinct is Correct — Dinner / Evening is the Best Time
Pumpkin seeds contain three sleep-promoting compounds that all work best when eaten 1–2 hours before bed:

🧠 Tryptophan — converts to serotonin → then melatonin (your sleep hormone). This conversion takes ~1–2 hours, so eating at dinner means it's working by sleep time.

💪 Zinc — supports tryptophan-to-melatonin conversion AND triggers overnight testosterone/muscle repair. Perfect timing: you go to sleep and zinc works all night rebuilding the muscles you trained.

😴 Magnesium — relaxes muscles and calms the nervous system. Reduces post-gym soreness overnight. Also lowers cortisol which would otherwise prevent fat burning during sleep.

Bottom line: pumpkin seeds at dinner = better sleep + overnight muscle repair + lower cortisol + more fat burned during sleep. All four goals in one 85-calorie tbsp.
🌙 BEST: At Dinner
Sprinkle 1 tbsp on your thoran at dinner. Eaten ~1–2 hours before sleep. Tryptophan converts to melatonin by bedtime. Zinc triggers overnight muscle repair. Magnesium relaxes body for deep sleep.
→ This is your plan going forward
✅ ALSO GOOD: Afternoon Snack
If you can't have at dinner (e.g. eating very late), 4pm snack is the second-best option. Still provides Zinc and Magnesium throughout the evening. Not as optimal for sleep timing but still excellent for recovery.
→ Backup option only
⚠️ OKAY: Morning
Provides energy and immune boost. Good for Zinc and Magnesium but the tryptophan converts to serotonin (daytime energy) instead of melatonin. Misses the overnight sleep and muscle repair benefits.
→ Not ideal for your goals
💡 Pro Tip
For maximum absorption: pair pumpkin seeds with a small amount of carbs (your thoran has some) or Skyr. This helps tryptophan cross the blood-brain barrier more effectively. Unsalted seeds are better — salted ones disrupt sleep with sodium.
📋 Your Pumpkin Seed Plan
Daily dose: 1 tbsp (15g) = 85 kcal · 4g protein · 7g fat · Zinc 2.5mg · Magnesium 75mg
When: Sprinkled on dinner thoran — every day
Type: Unsalted, dry roasted or raw — avoid salted/flavored varieties
Why at dinner specifically: You gym in morning or evening — either way, overnight is when muscles repair. Zinc and Magnesium from dinner seeds are available exactly when needed during deep sleep.
